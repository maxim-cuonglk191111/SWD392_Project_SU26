CEFR-aligned (Cambridge A1 → early A2) topic framework for STAGE 1 – BEGINNER (Levels 1–30) of LISA, focusing ONLY on SPEAKING, designed for gamification, daily-life relevance, and psychological safety for shy or low-confidence speakers.
This is written so that game designers, language designers, and AI engineers can all work from the same structure.
This structure is very important because:
•	It gives clear speaking tasks (no silence)
•	It keeps anxiety low
•	It allows AI prompts + Moderator guidance
•	It ensures 60 minutes = real speaking time
________________________________________
🎮 LISA – STAGE 1 (LEVELS 1–30)
Levels 1–30 | Target Outcome: Elementary Speaking (A1 → early A2)
Each level = 60 minutes speaking | 6 sub-levels × 10 minutes
Design principles (aligned with CEFR – Cambridge):
•	Concrete → familiar → personal
•	No abstract opinions
•	No debate
•	High repetition, low cognitive load
•	Emotionally safe, everyday topics
•	“I – My – Here – Now” orientation
Each level = 6 sub-levels × 10 minutes
Universal structure for EVERY 10-minute sub-level
1.	Warm-up (2 min) – repetition, safe phrases
2.	Core speaking (6 min) – personal + simple
3.	Wrap-up (2 min) – repeat / summarize

🔵 LEVELS 61–70 | STRONG B1 → B1+
🔵 LEVEL 61 – MANAGING STUDY & LIFE
1. My current study/work schedule
Describe real daily and weekly responsibilities clearly and logically.
Your current status (student, working, both)
o Typical weekday schedule: Morning/Afternoon/Evening activities
o Weekend schedule: How it is different from weekdays
o Fixed responsibilities: Classes, meetings, assignments, deadlines
o Flexible activities: Self-study, exercise, hobbies, rest
2. Time management methods
Explain how and why you organize your time in certain ways.
o Tools you use (planner, calendar app, to-do list)
o How you prioritize tasks: Important vs urgent
o How you plan a typical day or week
o Why you choose these methods
o What works well for you
3. Busy vs relaxed periods
Compare different time periods and describe contrasts clearly.
o Describe a very busy period: Exams, deadlines, projects
o Describe a relaxed period: Holidays, breaks, free weeks
o Compare: Stress level, Sleep, Mood and energy, Speaking or social time
o Which period you prefer and why
4. Time-related problems
Identify problems and explain their causes and effects.
o Common time problems you face: Lack of time, Too many tasks, Poor concentration
Situations where time pressure affects you
o Effects of poor time management: Stress, Lower performance, Less communication practice
o Why these problems happen
5. Improving balance
Reflect and give personal opinions about improvement.
o What balance means to you
o What you are doing well now
o What you need to improve
o Strategies to balance: Study/work, Rest, English speaking practice
o Advice you would give to someone with the same problem
6. Weekly life narrative
Start of the week (Monday mindset)
o Major responsibilities and challenges
o One difficult moment
o How you managed your time
o End of the week reflection
o What you learned about balance
